#  Functions of SAP Plant Maintenance (PM)

SAP Plant Maintenance (PM) performs three primary functions to help organizations maintain their plants, technical systems, and equipment effectively.

Each function includes various activities aimed at improving system reliability and operational efficiency.

---

## Inspection

Inspection focuses on determining the **current condition** of equipment and systems.

### Key Points

- Identifies wear and potential issues  
- Helps detect early signs of failure  
- Provides data for planning maintenance  

Outcome:
- Supports preventive maintenance  
- Reduces unexpected downtime  

---

##  Preventive Maintenance

Preventive maintenance ensures that equipment operates under **optimal conditions**.

### Key Points

- Scheduled maintenance activities  
- Regular servicing and checks  
- Avoids system deterioration  

Outcome:
- Increases equipment availability  
- Improves performance and reliability  

---

## Repair

Repair activities restore equipment or systems to their **ideal working condition**.

### Key Activities

- Fixing faults  
- Replacing damaged components  
- Creating purchase requisitions for spare parts  

 Outcome:
- Minimizes operational interruptions  
- Ensures system functionality is restored quickly  

---

## Additional Maintenance Activities

In addition to core functions, SAP PM supports other activities to streamline maintenance operations:

- Planning and scheduling maintenance tasks  
- Managing work orders and notifications  
- Tracking maintenance history  
- Optimizing resource usage  

Goal:
- Maximize plant and equipment uptime  
- Improve overall maintenance efficiency  

---

## Summary

- SAP PM focuses on **Inspection, Preventive Maintenance, and Repair**  
- Enables proactive and reactive maintenance strategies  
- Helps organizations achieve **high availability and reduced downtime**
