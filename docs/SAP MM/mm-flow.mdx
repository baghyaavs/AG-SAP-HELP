# SAP MM Flows and Business Processes

SAP Materials Management (MM) revolves around three primary flows that ensure smooth supply chain operations.

---

## Core Flows in SAP MM

### Material Flow

Material flow focuses on the movement of materials from suppliers to customers.

#### Flow Stages:

1. Raw materials are procured from suppliers  
2. Materials are processed into finished goods  
3. Products are distributed to customers  

- If customers return products, the material flow continues back to the manufacturer.

---

### Information Flow

Information flow deals with **order-related data and communication**.

#### Key Aspects:

- Order management  
- Delivery status tracking  
- Order turnaround time  

Importance:
- Provides transparency between business and customers  
- Enables real-time tracking and updates  

---

### Financial Flow

Financial flow focuses on the **valuation and accounting of materials**.

#### Key Areas:

- Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)  
- Inventory valuation  
- Accounts payable  

Importance:
- Ensures accurate financial reporting  
- Supports bookkeeping and cost control  

---

## Integration with Business Processes

SAP MM plays a critical role in multiple end-to-end business processes.

---

### Procure-to-Pay (P2P)

Also known as **Source-to-Pay**

#### Process:

1. Identify material requirements  
2. Request supplier quotations  
3. Select best vendor  
4. Procure materials  

#### Impacted Flows:

- Material Flow  
- Financial Flow  

---

### Plan-to-Product

Also called **Design-to-Operate**

#### Process:

1. Select raw and packaging materials  
2. Use inventory for production  
3. Manufacture finished goods  

#### Impacted Flows:

- Material Flow  
- Financial Flow  
- Includes production cost tracking  

---

### Order-to-Cash (O2C)

Also called **Lead-to-Cash**

#### Process:

1. Receive customer orders  
2. Fulfill and deliver products  
3. Process payments  

#### Impacted Flows:

- Material Flow  
- Information Flow  
- Financial Flow  

---

### Maintenance Management

Supports plant maintenance processes.

#### Key Activities:

- Procure spare parts  
- Store and manage inventory  
- Ensure availability during maintenance  

 Managed through:
- SAP Plant Maintenance  
- SAP S/4HANA Asset Management  

---

### Project Management

Supports capital projects such as plant expansion or equipment upgrades.

#### Key Activities:

- Procurement of:
  - Equipment  
  - Machinery  
  - Materials  

- Inventory tracking of project-related components  

SAP MM manages all associated material inventory.

## Summary

- SAP MM operates through **Material, Information, and Financial flows**  
- These flows integrate into core business processes like:
  - Procure-to-Pay  
  - Plan-to-Product  
  - Order-to-Cash
